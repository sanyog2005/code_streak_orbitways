FAQ's

  • What kind of training do I need before going to space?

    You will undergo a comprehensive training program that includes physical fitness, familiarization with the spacecraft, safety procedures, and simulations of the space environment.

  • How long does a typical space journey last?

    The duration of a space journey can vary from a few minutes of weightlessness on a suborbital flight to several days for orbital trips. Specific missions, like trips around the moon, may last up to a week or more.

  • What should I expect during takeoff and re-entry?

    You’ll experience increased G-forces as the spacecraft accelerates. It involves deceleration and can be accompanied by significant G-forces and heat buildup on the spacecraft’s exterior.

  • Can anyone go to space, or are there health requirements?

    Space travel requires a certain level of physical and mental fitness. Prospective travelers must pass a medical examination to ensure they can handle the stresses of spaceflight.

  • What should I pack for my trip to space?

    Space is limited on spacecraft, so packing is minimal. Essentials include personal items, comfortable clothing for microgravity, and any required medication. The rest will be provided by the space travel company.
